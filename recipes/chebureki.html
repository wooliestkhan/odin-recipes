Chebureki

Description

Cheburek! Best food from Russia! Fried pastry, so crunchy, inside meat is juicy with onion and spices. If you not try, you not live, my friend.

Ingredients

For dough:
  • 1 egg
  • 3 cups flour
  • 1 shot of vodka
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup water
  • Sunflower seed oil
For filling:
  • 400g/14oz beef or pork minced meat
  • Some green onion, parsley and dill
  • Some ground black pepper
  • 2 small onions
  • 1 clove garlic
  • 3 teaspoons salt

Steps

For dough:
  1. Add flour to large bowl
  2. Add egg, water, and sunflower seed oil
  3. Add salt and sugar
  4. Add shot of vodka (very important - do not skip this step!)
  5. Mix components well
  6. Grab the dough and roughly knead, until consistency of "melted alien brain" is reached. (Add more water/oil if too dry.)
For filling:
  1. Chop onions as finely as you can (do not use food processor!)
  2. Chop garlic
  3. Chop dill and parsley as finely as you can
  4. Add chopped onions, garlic, dill and parsley to a mixing bowl
  5. Add minced meat
  6. Add ground black pepper
  7. With your slav hands, mix filling
Assembly:
  1. Roll the dough into a "sausage" and cut into 6 equal sections
  2. Flatten one of the sections into a roughly 6 inch circle
  3. Add a ball of your filling to the center of the flattened dough (feel free to spread the filling around a little bit)
  4. Fold one side of the flattened dough to the other side, so it covers the filling.
  5. Make sure the edges are sufficiently thick to not allow filling to escape while frying.
  6. Use a fork to seal the edges of the cheburek
  7. Cut off any extra dough around the edges with a knife
  8. Repeat process for each section of dough.
Frying:
  1. Add sunflower seed oil to pan (enough to cover raw cheburek until it's about 75% covered)
  2. Set it to boil
  3. Once sufficiently hot, carefully place a few chebureki into the pan (reccomend using wooden spatula)
  4. Cook for a few minutes, until bottom of chebureki is golden brown
  5. Flip chebureki and cook other side until they're golden brown as well
  6. Place unto paper towel to dry
  7. (Optional: poke a few holes through the cheburek with a toothpick to allow oil to drain.)
  8. Serve with dark bread, horseradish, or mayonaise and enjoy!
